    • Color Management

    Tags: Idea, Desktop, Graphics, openSUSE, SLED, X11

    Description

    Gimp is a powerful graphic application with a lot of professional features. There are a lot of management tools for digital photos.

    But these tools can not be used professionally because there is no color management system available on Linux. You are not able to calibrate your monitor so you don't know whether the colors of your monitor fit to the colors of your photo lab. The manufacturer of your printer publishes an ICC profile but you are not able to import it on Linux. Low priced hardware for monitor calibration is available (e.g., ColorVision Spyder2) but only for Mac and Windows.

    People

    Michael Eicks originated this idea. No one is currently working on implementing this idea (it's up for grabs!).
