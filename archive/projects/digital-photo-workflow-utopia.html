    • Digital Photo Workflow Utopia

    Tags: Idea, openSUSE, SLED, HelpWanted, HW-Hacker, HW-Tester, HW-Designer, Desktop, Mono, Photography, Camera, F-Spot, GNOME, Conduit, Usability

    Description

    Problem: Dealing with my digital photos takes TOO LONG!
    Solution:

    - Take some pictures - Plug my camera in to my computer (yes, I'd still get a popup dialog asking me whether I want to import photos) - Photos are transferred from my camera to my computer - Review photos in full screen * During the review the app should let me * Delete obvious bad ones (blurry, etc.) * Rate the photos * Tag the photo (keywords, who's in the photo, etc.) * Rotate, adjust the exposure/white balance, fix red-eye, etc. * Place them in any of my pre-defined albums (or let me create a new album on the fly) - I'm DONE! * THIS step is where all the magic happens! * Some program (process) should be watching my every move in my photo tool and ... * When it detects that I've finished importing my photos (or really any time I modify the tags or organize my photos differently) it takes care of the most important thing...uploading/updating them to Flickr! * Converts RAW files to appropriately-sized JPEG files for upload (and then delete the JPEG files off my computer after the upload is finished) * Sets the permission on the photo automatically (public/private/family/friends) * This could be done based on the tags i add on the photo (with a smart default if the tag isn't there) * Adds the photo to a set (match it up with the album I already set up in my photo tool) * Match it up with the album I placed the photo in or a special tag/keyword (e.g., "Set:My Best Shots") * Extra Credit: New photos are automatically archived onto an external hard drive/server/etc.

    Get the idea? Get rid of manually exporting/uploading to Flickr!

    Technologies to Explore/Use

    * F-Spot * Conduit * Others? Please suggest!
